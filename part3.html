Community

CSUN ASA serves many different purposes for the Armenian students on campus, but perhaps it’s biggest responsibility is to foster and grow the Armenian community on the CSUN campus.

It is common knowledge that there is large concentration of Armenians in Glendale, and North Hollywood in southern California. Many Armenians who pursue college in those areas attend either Valley College or Glendale Community College. However, when they are finished with their two years at those junior colleges, they have to see which university close to home they’ll be accepted to.

For the most part, family is very important to Armenians so many of them choose not to move away to a far off college in order to stay closer to home. And to many Armenians, CSUN is one of their top choices, so there is a huge population of Armenians at CSUN. CSUN ASA has a very large responsibility to create an Armenian community at CSUN where Armenians can not only find friends, but can also seek life help and find professional connections as well.

“I never knew there were so many Armenians on campus.”

So far, the CSUN ASA has been doing a good job of building the community with its members by doing things like hosting fun events, and creating a group chat where members can always feel like they have friends within the Armenian community.

Overall the CSUN ASA is doing a great job building a relationship between the Armenian students on campus, and we all hope it continues to do so for years to come.
